# Market Creation Templates - Examples and Best Practices

## Template Examples by Category

### Crypto Price Predictions

These templates help users predict cryptocurrency price movements with clear market conditions.

#### Example 1: Simple Price Target

```
Question: Will Bitcoin reach $50,000 by December 31, 2024?
Template: Crypto Price Prediction
Category: Crypto
Duration: 2 Weeks

Resolution:
- YES: Bitcoin price reaches $50,000 on any major exchange
- NO: Bitcoin price remains below $50,000
Source: CoinMarketCap closing price
```

#### Example 2: Price Range

```
Question: Will Ethereum be trading between $2,000 and $3,000 on December 25, 2024?
Template: Crypto Price Prediction
Category: Crypto
Duration: 1 Month

Resolution:

- NO: ETH outside this range
Source: Kraken historical prices
```

#### Example 3: Market Dominance


## Best Practices

### Question Writing

#### Good Examples

✓ "Will Bitcoin reach $60,000 by December 31, 2024?"
- Clear asset
- Specific price level
- Specific date
- Binary outcome

✓ "Will the Fed lower interest rates at the December 2024 FOMC meeting?"
- Clear decision
- Specific timing
- Official source
- Binary outcome

✓ "Will the Lakers win at least 50 games in the 2024-25 NBA season?"
- Clear metric
- Specific timeframe
- Official source
- Measurable criterion

#### Problematic Examples

✗ "Will crypto be up?"
- Too vague
- Unclear timeframe
- Ambiguous metric

✗ "Will the election happen?"
- Too obvious
- No real prediction involved

✗ "Will Company X do well?"
- Subjective metric
- Hard to resolve objectively
- No clear threshold

### Category Selection

**Crypto** - Use for cryptocurrency prices, market metrics, blockchain events
**Sports** - Use for games, tournaments, player statistics, team outcomes
**Politics** - Use for elections, legislation, policy decisions, political events
**Economics** - Use for GDP, inflation, unemployment, economic indicators
**Technology** - Use for product launches, features, software updates, tech announcements
**Entertainment** - Use for awards, show renewals, chart performance, entertainment events
**Other** - Use for everything else that doesn't fit above categories

### Duration Selection

**1 Day (144 blocks)** - Same-day events, immediate outcomes
**3 Days (432 blocks)** - Weekly events, sports matches, short-term predictions
**1 Week (1008 blocks)** - Award ceremonies, weekly metrics, near-term events
**2 Weeks (2016 blocks)** - Economic reports, policy decisions, planned events
**1 Month (4320 blocks)** - Monthly indicators, quarterly deadlines, product launches
**3 Months (12,960 blocks)** - Quarterly indicators, seasonal events, major decisions

### Validation Tips

1. **Be Specific**: Use exact numbers, dates, and sources
2. **Make It Resolvable**: Ensure a clear, objective way to determine the outcome
3. **Reference Official Sources**: Use government agencies, official companies, credible data providers
4. **Avoid Ambiguity**: Don't use words like "might", "probably", "many"
5. **Include Timeframes**: Always specify "by [date]" or "in [period]"
6. **Use Clear Operators**: >, <, =, between, before, after
7. **Consider Edge Cases**: What happens if there's a tie, postponement, or controversy?

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Ambiguous Dates

❌ "Will Bitcoin reach $50,000 soon?"
✓ "Will Bitcoin reach $50,000 by December 31, 2024?"

### Mistake 2: Unclear Resolution Source

❌ "Will the stock market go up?"
✓ "Will the S&P 500 close above 5,000 on December 31, 2024?" (Source: Yahoo Finance)

### Mistake 3: Subjective Outcomes

❌ "Will the new Marvel movie be good?"
✓ "Will the new Marvel movie earn $1 billion at the global box office?" (Source: Box Office Mojo)

### Mistake 4: Vague Measurements

❌ "Will unemployment improve?"
✓ "Will unemployment remain below 4.5% in February 2025?" (Source: BLS)

### Mistake 5: Too Distant Timeframe

❌ "Will Bitcoin reach $1 million?" (no timeframe)
✓ "Will Bitcoin reach $1 million by December 31, 2030?"

### Mistake 6: Dependent on Unrelated Events

❌ "Will crypto go up if the Fed cuts rates?"
✓ "Will Bitcoin reach $50,000 by June 30, 2025?"

## Advanced Examples

### Conditional Markets

```
Question: If Team A wins the playoff, will they win the championship?
- Better as two separate markets
- Market 1: Will Team A win the playoff?
- Market 2: Will Team A win the championship?
```

### Multi-Condition Markets

```
Question: Will both Bitcoin exceed $50k AND Ethereum exceed $3k by Dec 2024?
- Better as single market with clear AND logic
- Resolution: Both conditions must be true for YES
```

### Percentage-Based Markets

```
Question: Will inflation be 2-3% in 2024 Q4?
- Use clear ranges
- Source the official measurement method
- Specify which inflation metric (CPI, PCE, etc.)
```

## Testing Your Question

Before creating, ask yourself:

1. Can someone who wasn't familiar with this resolve it objectively?
2. Is there a single, authoritative source for the answer?
3. Could the outcome be ambiguous or disputed?
4. Is the timeframe reasonable for resolution?
5. Does the question avoid personal opinion?
6. Can you explain the YES and NO outcomes clearly?

If you hesitate on any of these, refine your question further.
